Production selections use net edge: model edge less bootstrap uncertainty. A selection needs more than 4 net points; it is 2 units above 8 net points. The rolling ledger uses the same production model and stakes, but its sample is small and is not a promise of future returns.

The model sees public stats only: Elo, age, layoffs, striking and grappling rates, accumulated damage, recent knockouts. It cannot see injuries, camp changes, weight cuts, or tape. Debutants and fighters returning from very long layoffs are outside its reliable range. Gold-bordered rows in Recent results were qualified bets.

This is a statistical model, not a tip sheet. There is no verified live-money edge. The event-clustered validation interval and forward CLV sample are the relevant gates, and both workflows remain paper-only. Any single card is noise.
